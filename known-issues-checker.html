Cervélo P2 2014–2019 · triathlon

Documented, source-checked issues to look for before you buy a used Cervélo P2. Every point below is verified against a forum thread, recall notice, or service bulletin — not opinion.

Recall check
Model-year boundary check — pre-2014 Cervelos carry old recalls (Wolf SL fork 2008; 3T Aura Pro bars 2012-13). If the seller is vague about year, verify against Cervelo's recall page before assuming this listing is the 2014+ generation.
Check in person: Confirm the model year from the serial number (BB shell underside) and check it against the Cervelo product-recalls page. 2014-2019 P2s are not in these recalls.
Ask the seller to photograph: Serial number on the underside of the bottom bracket shell.
source
Structural
Cracked seatpost collar / top of seat tube — documented on the earlier P2C/P3C generation; worth a careful look on any used Cervelo aero frame at the same stress point.
Check in person: Inspect the top of the seat tube and clamp area in good light for hairline cracks radiating from the clamp slot. Flex the saddle side-to-side and listen.
Ask the seller to photograph: Top of the seat tube / clamp slot from behind, in daylight, close focus.
source
Maintenance
BBright press-fit bottom bracket creak/knock — resin high spots behind the bearing seat cause frequent creaking; repeated bearing presses wear the shell loose. A thread-together BB (Wheels Mfg/BBInfinite) is the accepted permanent fix and a plus if already installed.
Check in person: Pedal hard out of the saddle in a big gear and listen for creak or knock from the cranks. Ask the seller if the BB has ever been replaced and with what — a thread-together conversion is good news; multiple re-pressed OEM bearings is a wear flag.
Ask the seller to photograph: Close-up of the bottom bracket shell from both sides, cranks in place is fine.
source
Maintenance
Seatpost slipping under load — a long-running P2 complaint: the clamp-only contact lets the post work down during rides; some cases traced to clamp screws bottoming out before full clamping force.
Check in person: Check for a saddle-height marker line on the post; look for vertical scoring on the post below the clamp = history of slipping. Ask whether carbon assembly paste is in use and torque-check the clamp bolts at the viewing.
Ask the seller to photograph: Seatpost at the collar, close enough to show any scratches or slip marks below the clamp line.
source

Documented, source-checked issues to look for before you buy a used Cervélo P3. Every point below is verified against a forum thread, recall notice, or service bulletin — not opinion.

Recall check
Model-year boundary recalls — P3-specific history: Wolf SL fork recall (2008 era P3 Carbon) and 3T Aura Pro handlebar recall (P3 Ultegra sold Sep 2012 - Jan 2013). A 2014-2019 P3 is clear, but sellers frequently misstate years — verify serial.
Check in person: Confirm model year from the serial (under the BB). If it could be 2013 or earlier: check the fork legs for "Wolf Superlite/SL" markings and the bars for 3T Aura Pro, and run the serial past the CPSC/Cervelo recall pages.
Ask the seller to photograph: Serial number under the BB shell + one photo of the fork crown/leg markings.
source
Structural
Cracked seatpost collar / top of seat tube — documented on the earlier P3C generation; same stress point exists on later frames, so inspect it regardless.
Check in person: Inspect the top of the seat tube and clamp slot in good light for hairline cracks. Flex the saddle and listen for ticking.
Ask the seller to photograph: Top of the seat tube / clamp slot from behind, close focus, daylight.
source
Maintenance
BBright press-fit bottom bracket creak/knock — documented even on brand-new P3s; resin high spots behind the bearing seat and repeated bearing presses wear the shell. Thread-together BB conversion (Wheels Mfg/BBInfinite) is the accepted permanent fix.
Check in person: Pedal hard out of the saddle in a big gear and listen for creak or knock from the crank area. Ask if the BB was ever converted to thread-together (good) or has had multiple pressed bearings (wear flag).
Ask the seller to photograph: Close-up of the bottom bracket shell from drive and non-drive side.
source
Maintenance
Seatpost slipping — same wedge/clamp design family as the P2; multiple owner threads. One documented root cause: clamp screws too long, bottoming out before the clamp bites.
Check in person: Look for vertical scoring on the post below the clamp line (slip history). Torque-check the clamp at the viewing; ask about carbon paste.
Ask the seller to photograph: Seatpost at the collar showing the area just below the clamp.

Specialized Shiv 2014–2021 · triathlon

Documented, source-checked issues to look for before you buy a used Specialized Shiv. Every point below is verified against a forum thread, recall notice, or service bulletin — not opinion.

Recall check
Aerobar recall on early Shiv Tri models — CPSC recalled ~8,300 Specialized aerobars (Mar 2015): the extension clamp bolt that attaches the extensions to the base bar can break or slip. Applies to aerobars sold aftermarket AND as original equipment on MY2012-2015 Shiv Tri. No frame recall found for the 2014-2021 tri frame (CPSC search checked 2026-07-05).
Check in person: If the bike is a 2014 or 2015 (or has the original aerobars from any 2012-2015 Shiv): ask whether the recall repair kit (replacement clamp bolts) was done; check the extension clamp bolts for slip/movement by loading the extensions firmly. 2016+ bars and replaced aftermarket extensions are outside the recall.
Ask the seller to photograph: Close-up of the aerobar extension clamps where the extensions meet the base bar.
source
Structural
Seatpost clamp receiving-nut pulling out of the frame — the metal nut bonded into the carbon that the clamp screw threads into can work loose/pull out; epoxy repairs often do not hold. Affects multiple model years.
Check in person: Look into the seatpost clamp area: the two metal receiving nuts should sit flush and even — one sitting further out than the other means it is pulling out. Torque the clamp bolts gently and watch for movement of the nuts.
Ask the seller to photograph: Straight-down close-up of the seatpost clamp showing both bolt/nut seats at the top of the seat tube.
source
Maintenance
Fuelselage internal hydration system issues — bladder/valve wear, mold in neglected bladders, and missing parts; replacements are pricey and increasingly scarce. A complete, clean Fuelselage kit meaningfully changes the bike's value.
Check in person: Open the top-tube compartment: check the bladder for mold/stiffness, the hose and bite valve condition, and confirm all pieces are present. Ask when the bladder was last replaced.
Ask the seller to photograph: The opened Fuelselage compartment with bladder and hose laid out.
source
Maintenance
Fuelcell (rear storage box) fit issues — reported falling off mid-ride, and on some setups low saddle positions interfere with the Fuelcell mount flag. Fuelcell fits the Shiv tri frame, NOT the Shiv TT — buyers regularly confuse the two bikes.
Check in person: First confirm which bike this is: Shiv tri (this report) vs Shiv TT — parts do not interchange. If a Fuelcell is included, check its latch engagement firmly and look for wear at the mount.
Ask the seller to photograph: Rear of the bike showing the Fuelcell mounted, plus one photo of it removed showing the mount points.
source

Trek Speed Concept 2014–2021 · triathlon

Documented, source-checked issues to look for before you buy a used Trek Speed Concept. Every point below is verified against a forum thread, recall notice, or service bulletin — not opinion.

Recall check
Recall boundary check — the 2022 Speed Concept SLR carbon basebar recall (37 broken-bar reports) does NOT cover the 2014-2021 gen, but sellers mislabel generations. If the listing claims 2022+/SLR/disc brakes, verify against CPSC before applying this report's comps.
Check in person: Rim brakes + external basebar = Gen 2 (2014-2021, this report). Disc brakes/SLR = 2022+ generation; check the CPSC recall and re-price separately.
Ask the seller to photograph: Full side-on photo of the bike clearly showing brake type.
source
Structural
BB90 bottom bracket carbon seat wear — bearings sit directly in molded carbon; the shell wears until bearings fall out by hand. Trek's fix is oversized "V2" (+0.1mm) bearings; once those are loose too, the frame needs a shop-level repair (e.g. BBInfinite sleeve). The single biggest used-purchase risk on this bike.
Check in person: Drop the chain to the small ring, grab both crank arms and push side-to-side feeling for play at the BB; spin the cranks and listen for grinding/creak. Ask directly: how many bearing replacements, and were V2 oversized bearings ever needed (that means the seats are already worn).
Ask the seller to photograph: Drive-side and non-drive-side close-ups of the bottom bracket area; if the seller will do it, one with the crank arm shadowing the bearing visible.
source
Maintenance
Proprietary fit hardware — Gen 2 uses proprietary stems (multiple lengths/rises) and a mono-extension; spares and the aftermarket 22.2mm mono-adapter are scarce. If the current stem/extension setup does not fit the buyer, correcting it can be slow and expensive.
Check in person: Confirm the current stem size and pad/extension position roughly matches the buyer's fit before purchase — treat a wrong-size front end as a real cost, not a tweak. Ask which stem length/rise is installed and whether any spare hardware comes with the bike.
Ask the seller to photograph: Side-on photo of the whole front end (stem, basebar, extensions) plus any spare fit parts included.
source
Maintenance
Integrated center-pull brakes + rear speedfin — fiddly to set up and keep centered; the rear speedfin cover draws complaints including tire rub under hard efforts.
Check in person: Squeeze both brakes hard and watch release/centering; check the rear (under-BB area) cabling for smooth pull. Look for rub marks on the rear tire sidewall and the inside of the speedfin.
Ask the seller to photograph: Rear brake area including the speedfin from behind, plus the rear tire sidewalls.
source
