2. What to inspect on this exact model

SeverityIssue — and how to check it in person
RECALLModel-year boundary recalls — P3-specific history: Wolf SL fork recall (2008 era P3 Carbon) and 3T Aura Pro handlebar recall (P3 Ultegra sold Sep 2012 - Jan 2013). A 2014-2019 P3 is clear, but sellers frequently misstate years — verify serial.
Confirm model year from the serial (under the BB). If it could be 2013 or earlier: check the fork legs for "Wolf Superlite/SL" markings and the bars for 3T Aura Pro, and run the serial past the CPSC/Cervelo recall pages. source
STRUCTURALCracked seatpost collar / top of seat tube — documented on the earlier P3C generation; same stress point exists on later frames, so inspect it regardless.
Inspect the top of the seat tube and clamp slot in good light for hairline cracks. Flex the saddle and listen for ticking. source
MAINTENANCEBBright press-fit bottom bracket creak/knock — documented even on brand-new P3s; resin high spots behind the bearing seat and repeated bearing presses wear the shell. Thread-together BB conversion (Wheels Mfg/BBInfinite) is the accepted permanent fix.
Pedal hard out of the saddle in a big gear and listen for creak or knock from the crank area. Ask if the BB was ever converted to thread-together (good) or has had multiple pressed bearings (wear flag). source
MAINTENANCESeatpost slipping — same wedge/clamp design family as the P2; multiple owner threads. One documented root cause: clamp screws too long, bottoming out before the clamp bites.
Look for vertical scoring on the post below the clamp line (slip history). Torque-check the clamp at the viewing; ask about carbon paste. source

4. Fit check

The listing is a 48cm, and your current road bike is in the 48–51 range, so the size is inside your window on paper — but we don't yet have the P3's stack/reach table in our database, so we won't guess at numbers. Do this instead: measure (or look up) the stack and reach of your current bike, then compare against the 48cm P3's figures on Cervélo's support page for the P3 (linked in our model notes) or any Pro's Closet P3 listing, which carry the full geometry chart.

Two things to check at the viewing itself: (1) standover — a tri frame's top tube sits differently from your road bike's; (2) the position of the aerobar pads with the current stem/spacer setup, since that, more than the frame size, decides whether a 160cm rider can get comfortable on this bike without buying new hardware.

5. Negotiation script

Opener: "I've looked at what P3s have actually sold for recently — the median is about $1,500, and the one sale that matches this bike exactly, a 48cm with Ultegra Di2, went for just over $2,000 CAD in late May. I like the bike, and the Zipp wheels add real value, but I can't start from $3,500. I can offer $2,200."

Concessions to ask for, tied to the checklist:

  • "Can you show me the serial under the bottom bracket? The ad says around 2015 — if we can't confirm the year, I'd need another $150 off, because pre-2014 P3s carry recall checks I'd have to do myself."
  • "Mind if I pedal hard out of the saddle for a minute? If the bottom bracket creaks, a proper thread-together fix is a shop job — I'd ask you to come down by what that costs, or have it done first."
  • "Can I pull the seatpost? If there's scoring below the clamp line it's been slipping, and I'd factor a shop visit into my offer."

Walk-away: "If $2,500 doesn't work today, no hard feelings — the recent sold prices just don't support more, and I'm happy to keep looking."

6. Notes on the listing itself

Three things in the listing text worth pinning down before you drive over. The "~2015" year comes from a linked reference, not from the seller's own words — get the serial number to confirm it (it also clears the recall check in section 2). "New battery" is a claim about the Di2 system — ask when it was replaced and whether both shifting and charging work through a full run of the gears at the viewing. And the ad says "Zipp wheels" without a model — Zipp's range is wide, so ask which model; it matters for what the bike is worth. Also note: no pedals are included, so budget for those.
